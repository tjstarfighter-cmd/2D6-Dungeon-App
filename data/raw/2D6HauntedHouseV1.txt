Introduction 
 
This is the Haunted House Expansion 
for 2D6 Dungeon, a seasonal special, 
and features the rules and tables for 
exploring a Haunted House. To play 
the Haunted House Expansion you 
will need the 2D6 Dungeon Core 
Rules to reference, but there are some 
exclusive and themed rules below that 
make exploring the Haunted House 
quite different to exploring a dungeon. 
You will also need the Tables Codex as 
some of the tables referenced in this 
expansion are found there.  
 
Warning 
 
There are aspects of horror in this 
version of 2D6 which you may find 
disturbing. It is also ‘set on hard’ and  
is meant to be challenging. 
 
Setting and Theme 
 
The Haunted House expansion is 
based in the same world as the rest of 
the 2D6 Dungeon and, although the 
Haunted House theme is often seen in 
modern contexts, in this situation it is 
placed in the fantasy world of 
Coradine. This time though, rather 
than playing an adventurer, you play 
the town constable and have been 
called to a house on the outskirts of 
town, where something strange is 
happening. As it happens, it is All 
Deaths' Eve and locals want the site 
cleared so the evil does not spread to 
the rest of the village. They are calling 
it a haunting, an infestation of Low 
Magic, but who knows what creatures 
have taken up residence? 
 
Low Magic 
 
There is a form of magic that is 
commonly known as ‘Low Magic’ and 
is viewed as debased and sinister by the 
normal folk of Coradine. It 
occasionally creeps to the surface, 
residuals of dark magic lingering on 
corpses of monsters who have 
breached the upside, contaminating 
objects that people then take up. 
Sometimes those who return from the 
dungeons unknowingly bring back 
cursed items or treasure and the power 
of the gods from below can seep into 
dark hollows or tar pits on open 
ground. A few who encounter these, 
occasionally women out foraging in the 
forests or children playing in the fields, 
become poisoned. They begin to 
practice the dark arts and cannot 
explain their compulsions. Over time 
they show signs of their corruption 
and are cast out, and often retreat into 
the underworld, or find a place of 
sanctuary away from prying eyes where 
they can continue with their newfound 
addiction. The magic they practice is 
weak and often involves imbuing 
strangely crafted objects with curses or 
wards, carving or weaving into them 
symbols with a dark purpose.

All Deaths' Eve 
 
On All Deaths' Eve those who practice 
Low Magic are drawn to each other in 
what is known as the Dark Correlation. 
It is a day when the vibrancy and heat 
of summer has long gone and ahead 
lies the cold of death. It is a turning 
point when their dark magic is not 
interrupted by the light, when 
creatures charged by Low Magic can 
emerge for one night before their 
power begins to wane or they are 
found. If they are left to practice their 
magic, they become stronger and next 
year the infection would have spread 
and, although they never threaten the 
peace of the surface, they can take 
your loved ones. 
 
The Haunted House is one such place 
where the Dark Correlation is 
happening, and it is up to you to stop 
the infection. 
 
The Constable 
 
In this version of 2D6 Dungeon you 
play a constable rather than an 
adventurer. You use a normal character 
sheet and use the same manoeuvres. 
The main difference between the 
adventurer and constable is the XP 
level names and number of them. The 
constable also never goes down into 
dungeons or lairs. They work on the 
surface and can be employed by 
adventurers to help deal with problems 
in their realm, an aspect that features 
in the expansion 2D6 Realm 
(published 2024). 
 
The Fear Sheet 
 
One of the few psychological aspects 
that Low Magic can command is 
primal fear. When you enter the 
Haunted House, you will encounter a 
focused and pervasive fear that is 
amplified by Low Magic. This will 
affect your mind and torture your 
physical being. To represent this there 
is an addition to your character sheet 
that is called the Fear Sheet, which you 
must print off for this adventure. It is 
found on page 12 after the rules.

How Fear Works 
 
You will gather Fear Points as you 
explore the Haunted House. As you do 
you become more crippled by fear, and 
it affects your ability to perform. When 
you gain Fear Points you are affected 
in numerous ways, as can be seen on 
the Fear Sheet. Your observational 
skills are marred, your ability to fight 
stifled and ultimately your sanity is 
tested by what you experience. The 
second you gain 10 Fear Points you 
crack, run your hands through your 
hair and flee from the house 
screaming. You have failed and spend 
time recovering over the coming 
weeks. If you can avoid 10 Fear Points 
and clear the final room, then the fear 
evaporates with the realisation of 
success. Archive the Fear Sheet. 
 
You can lose Fear Points when in the 
Haunted House, but there are only a 
few occasions when you do. Fear Point 
effects are also cumulative, so if you 
have 6 points then you have 6 affects 
that are imposed on your character, the 
effect for the sixth point and all the 
points prior to that. 
 
Magic Banes 
 
On the Fear Sheet is a space for Magic 
Banes. These are objects, imbued with 
Low Magic, that you have found in the 
house. All of them have a one-time use 
and have been left in the house by the 
Low Magic users who infest it. They 
can be used on their creators though 
and are useful when facing tough odds. 
But there is a side effect, if you use 
one you gain 1 Fear Point, the effect 
of the Magic Bane terrifying you (see 
Magic Bane table). Banes cannot be 
taken outside the house. They have no 
power if they are.  
 
Designed for Low Level Characters 
 
The Haunted House is designed for 
level 1 constables. A starting adventure 
if you like for this new character. If 
you wish to explore a second Haunted 
House with this character, then double 
the number of creatures in every 
second encounter apart from the loft. 
It is designed to be a short Halloween 
adventure with a small, three level map. 
As previously mentioned there will be 
further missions for the constable in 
future expansions. 
 
Size of the Map 
 
There are three levels to the Haunted 
House. The first two are 11x11 and the 
third and top level, the loft space, is 
one room size. The stairs on levels 1 
and 2 go upwards as would be 
expected in a house. There is no cellar. 
 
Completing the House 
 
The goal for the Haunted House is to 
clear all three levels. The final room is 
in the loft space, and you need to 
explore every room on the map to 
clear out all the monsters that live 
there so they will no longer threaten 
the town. Once this is done the 
constable descends and leaves the 
house. Back in town the people are 
relieved and you are given 30 GC, a

8 
small reward that has been gathered as 
thanks for their continued safety. The 
local bard is already scribing a song of 
your exploits.  
 
Trapped In The House 
 
When you enter the house the door 
slams shut behind you and is held shut 
by a magical presence, Low Magic 
ward runes scratched upon its surface. 
You cannot force it open or retreat 
through a window as they too are 
magically sealed. Only when you clear 
the house does the magic dissipate, 
allowing you to escape, or when you 
are broken by fear and it releases you. 
 
Treasure 
 
There is a range of All Deaths’ Eve 
themed loot and several special items 
to be found in the Haunted House. All 
are yours to keep, apart from Banes, as 
part of your constable’s salary. 
 
Gods 
 
The gods have no hold over the 
Haunted House, their presence kept at 
bay by the Low Magic that is 
empowered by All Deaths’ Eve. In the 
Haunted House you are alone. 
 
Drawing Creature Encounters 
 
There is more flexibility with creature 
encounters in the Haunted House. 
There are four Halloween level 1 
animals (HL1A), four Halloween level 
1 Humanoids (HL1H) and four 
Halloween level 1 undead (HL1U) 
included here and can be found at the 
back of the book. There is also a free 
Halloween Creature Card Expansion 
for the Haunted House with more 
creatures that can be added. This is 
free to download and includes 
contributions from the original backers 
of 2D6 Dungeon. When asked to roll 
on a creature table for the Haunted 
House you can instead draw a card of 
the correct type if you prefer, turn it 
over and face the creature. In this way 
you can expand the range of creatures 
available. 
 
Tables 
 
Some core rules tables are referenced 
in the room descriptions, for which 
you will need to use the Tables Codex. 
These include HST1, RATT1, POT1, 
POT2, PT1, BT1, SCT1 and SCT2. 
 
No Patrols and Sound 
 
There are no patrols in the Haunted 
House, but if you do make excessive 
noise, you hear a loud scream and stop 
what you are doing. You gain 1 Fear 
Point consequently. It pays to be quiet 
in the housed house. 
 
No Rations 
 
You may not eat or use rations in the 
house. You do not eat on the stairs, for 
the levels are considered to be 1 level.

Room and Door Generation 
Changes 
 
There are several key changes to how 
rooms and doors are generated in the 
Haunted House. This is because the 
function of the property is very 
different from a dungeon and 
consequently work differently, 
although these changes require you to 
know the core rules so you can adapt 
them. 
 
Considered as 1 Level 
 
Even though there are 3 levels to the 
house compared to dungeon sizes they 
are much smaller. All three are 
considered 1 level for potion and scroll 
effects. For example, if you take a 
Potion of Willpower then it lasts for 
the whole time you are in the house. 
 
Doors and Locks 
 
Every door in the Haunted House is 
wooden. Draw them in as you go. 
They are also unlocked but they have a 
mind of their own, the Low Magic 
seeping into the once living wood of 
the structure. When using a door roll a 
D6. On a 5 or 6 you roll on HDT, the 
Haunted Door Table, for something 
strange is going on. Do not roll on 
HDT when back tracking. 
 
A Door on Every Wall 
 
Whenever you generate a room in the 
Haunted House add an exit to every 
wall unless one of the walls is the 
Outer Boundary. Add a door even if 
the wall has only one available square. 
Do not add more than one door to a 
wall and, as per normal, do not add a 
door to the wall you entered through. 
There is one exception though. If 
there is a space that can only be 
accessed if you place a second door 
on the adjoining wall, then do so. 
No space can go unexplored in the 
house, even single squares. 
 
No Exit Squares 
 
There are no exit shafts in the Haunted 
House, for the walls are narrow 
wooden partitions, not thick stone 
walls that you find in a dungeon. The 
rooms are tightly packed to take full 
advantage of the foundation. Simply 
incorporate the walls you have already 
established into any new rooms you 
generate.  
 
Fitting in Rooms 
 
You must fill the entire floor space of 
the house. If you generate a room that 
is larger than the space but there is a  
===== PAGE 10 =====
10 
 gap next to another room that it may 
push into, do so and occupy this off-
set space. This represents the fact that 
some of these rooms have alcoves and 
inbuilt nooks. Failure to fit in small 
spaces to the natural room layout will 
result in too many small rooms. 
 
No Generated Corridors (+1 to 1s) 
 
Apart from the Entrance Room there 
are no corridors in the Haunted 
House. When you roll a corridor +1 
to the 1 (not to both dice unless you 
roll double 1) and so therefore treat it 
as a small room. Make sure you 
generate every small space available in 
the house, even single squares. 
 
No Large Rooms 
 
There are no, what are considered by 
the core rules, large rooms in the 
Haunted House. Consider all rooms, 
apart from small rooms, which are 6 
squares or smaller, to be normal 
rooms. Roll on the Level Room Table. 
If you roll doubles proceed as normal 
and roll again. Even if the dimensions 
of the room are large, still roll for a 
normal room, although there is limited 
space in the house. 
 
Types of  Room 
 
For each level there are a maximum of 
18 different types of normal room, per 
level, rather than 36 as in the core rules 
for dungeons. This is simply because 
there are only so many types of room 
found in a house on Coradine. It is 
also worth noting that not all houses 
have a kitchen because inhabitants may 
cook over a fireplace or burner. But 
due to the presence of Low Magic you 
cannot start fires yourself in the house. 
The elemental properties are not the 
same in this haunted abode. Each 
room has two dice number references 
rather than one and each room is 
unique, not including small rooms. 
 
Level 1 - The Entrance Room 
Hallway 
 
The first room of the Haunted House 
is a 1 by 6 corridor, called the hallway, 
with two evenly spaced wooden doors 
along the east and west walls (two on 
each wall) with a staircase going up to 
level 2 at the end of the corridor, 
giving you five ways in which to leave 
the hallway. Add in the stairs up from 
level 1, so you will need to position 
this new room above the stairs below. 
There is no encounter here. You can 
explore the rooms in any order but you 
must clear all rooms to complete the 
adventure.

Level 2 – The Landing 
 
When you climb the stairs to level 2, 
add in the stairs from level 1, so you 
will need to position the level 2 landing 
room above the stairs below. Including 
the stairs from level 1, draw a 2 by 5, 
or 5 by 2, room in any direction you 
like. Add a staircase or ladder in one 
corner that leads to level 3. There is no 
encounter here. Draw a wooden door 
on each wall.  
 
Level 3 – The Loft Space 
 
When you enter this level simply 
generate 1 room but plus 3 to each 
dice, making the room larger. Roll 
for this on LFTT1 - Loft Space Table 1 
to discover what it is. There is only one 
room on this level. It is the final room 
and when cleared, as long as all the 
rooms on the lower levels are also 
cleared, you have completed the 
Haunted House. The front door opens, 
the Low Magic dissipates, and you can 
leave victorious.

This is the Haunted House Expansion for 2D6 Dungeon, a seasonal special, 
and features the rules and tables for exploring a Haunted House. To play the 
Haunted House Expansion you will need the 2D6 Dungeon Core Rules to 
reference, but there are some exclusive and themed rules that make exploring 
the Haunted House quite different to exploring a dungeon. You will also need 
the Tables Codex as some of the tables referenced in this expansion are found 
there.  
 
The Haunted House expansion is based in the same world as the rest of the 
2D6 Dungeon and although the Haunted House theme is often seen in modern 
contexts, in this situation it is placed in the fantasy world of Coradine. This time 
though, rather than playing an adventurer, you play the town constable and have 
been called to a house on the outskirts of town, where something strange is 
happening. As it happens it is All Deaths' Eve and locals want the site cleared so 
the evil does not spread to the rest of the village. They are calling it a haunting, 
an infestation of Low Magic, but who knows what creatures have taken up 
residence?

Introduction 
 
This is the Lairs Expansion for 2D6 
Dungeon and features the rules and 
tables for the Infernal Caves and 
Sunken Swamp. To play the Lairs 
Expansion you will need the 2D6 
Dungeon Core Rules and any 
additional rules are listed below. You 
will also need the Tables Codex as 
some of the tables referenced in this 
expansion are found there. 
 
A New Adventure 
 
Adventurers are known to have braved 
the caves for many reasons. To help rid 
the land of the creatures that might 
venture out at night or to find treasure 
to support their growing domains. 
Others enter to raise their level before 
they venture back into the main 
undefeated dungeon that plagues the 
land. 
 
Difficulty Level 
 
These lairs can be taken on by any 
level adventurer, levels 1 to 10. There 
are special conditions that have to be 
applied depending on your level. For 
example, when you encounter traps, 
you will take damage per level of 
adventurer. There are also four 
versions of each creature. You fight 
the creature that is closest to your level 
at the start of the combat. If it is a tie 
and you have fifty percent or less of 
your HP you face the lower level 
enemy. There are a few minor 
exceptions, creatures which do not 
have variations. Just fight these as per 
their normal creature card from the 
Tables Codex. 
 
Size of the Lair 
 
Every lair you explore cannot exceed 
25x25 squares. This size is set for 
balancing reasons. 
 
Dead Ends 
 
If you find that you face a series of 
dead ends, cannot progress and there is 
less than 80% of the Lair explored, 
then add in an extra room exit. Select a 
wall that leads into as wide a space as 
possible. This secret exit has revealed 
itself after extensive searches.  
 
Completing a Lair 
 
You may retreat from a lair at any 
point, following the same path you 
came in by. But you cannot re-enter 
and carry on where you left off. You 
would instead restart a new lair. 
 
The goal for the Infernal Caves is to 
kill Harakor. Once this is done there is 
an exit in that room that will take you 
out to the surface. In the Sunken 
Swamp you need to explore every 
room and clear out all the monsters 
that live there so they will no longer 
threaten the town. Once this is done 
the final room presents an exit you can 
take. Back in town there is praise for 
what you have achieved and word soon 
spreads. You are toasted in the pub and 
awarded a purse of 100 GC by the 
town mayor.

An Example Incomplete Lair 
 
In this example the adventurer, Level 2 
Vel Cotez, entered a sunken swamp 
and made their way through a series of 
rooms. They encountered a 
Bladderwort Host, that took them 
close to death. They pushed on 
looking for treasure and came to a 
Talis Lotus in a room full of tall plants. 
They fought valiantly and managed to 
take down the creature with only 2 HP 
remaining. Being weakened, soaked 
and on their last legs Vel left the lair 
and returned to the surface. Having 
not completed the lair they did not 
receive a reward from the mayor and 
cannot pick this lair back up in the 
future. She will never find this lair 
again.

You can always return to these lairs 
and explore them again once they have 
been completed because the Order 
restock the caves and new creatures 
eventually emerge in the swamp. 
 
No Exit Shafts 
 
There are no exit shafts to climb or 
portals to find in the lairs. There is 
only one way in and only one way out, 
but as previously mentioned, you  can 
retreat from a lair. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Treasure 
 
Loot quantity and quality varies 
depending on the level of the creature 
you are fighting, even if you are facing 
a different variation of the same 
creature. Also, when you find treasure, 
it will sometimes have a new 
abbreviation. PL refers to Per Level of 
Adventurer and is placed after the coin 
amount such as in this example: 10 GC 
PL. Here if you are level 2 you would 
find 20 GC and if you were level 9 you 
would find 90 GC. This is for balance 
purposes. Not all treasure is adjusted 
like this. 
 
Damage 
 
The PL abbreviation is also used in 
relation to damage taken on occasion. 
This reflects that the encounter is no 
doubt tougher for the higher level 
dungeons, which are more financially 
rewarding. 
  
The Gods 
 
The Gods have little physical presence 
in lairs, for the creatures that lurk there 
are not worshippers. But they can be 
tapped into if the right situation 
presents itself and occasions might 
occur when they could aid you on your 
journey. 
 
Cave and Swamp Walls 
 
The walls in the caves and swamps are 
uneven. As a result, when you are 
drawing the walls for the rooms make 
the line uneven to represent this. These 
wiggly lines have to remain within one 
square width of the rolled dimensions 
of the room.

8 
If at any point a new room crosses 
over this rough boundary, as rooms 
will sometimes butt up to other rooms, 
redraw both walls so they share the 
buffer zone. 
Doors and Locks 
 
There are no doors in the Sunken 
Swamp or the Infernal Caves so all 
exits are archways. There are chests 
though and some will be locked. This 
will be highlighted in the text. 
 
The Infernal Caves 
 
To the north of the village is a network 
of caves, the entrance for which is 
known about and avoided. At night 
terrifying calls can be heard emanating 
from the depths. It is said that the 
Infernal Order release the animals they 
experiment on into the cave system, 
creatures they cannot contain in their 
Menagerie, but may have a use for in 
some future maniacal plan. The caves 
are formed by the heat of a large 
underground lava lake and so are the 
perfect environment for the creatures. 
As a result, the Order have hidden 
treasure within the caves to lure 
Overlanders to their death. Rumour 
has it that a monstrous, mutated bear 
lives within and such is its reputation 
that it has become known as Harakor. 
The cave is also rumoured to be 
infested with Furnicar, giant, heat 
loving, human-hybrid ants.  
 
Fire Resistance 
 
The Infernal Monks have cast a 
magical aura over the caves which 
nullifies rings and armour that have 
fire resistance. They only have no 
effect for the Infernal Caves.  
 
The Sunken Swamp 
 
To the west of the village is the 
wetlands. Fed by a series of narrow 
tributaries the area is now flooded, old 
fishing hamlets lost and sections have 
formed swamps and marshes. 
Treacherous to navigate, few but the 
Osier farmers and Fisher People dare 
to enter. Recently undead beings and 
mutated creatures have been emerging 
from the swamps and rumour has it 
that some kind of subterranean lair is 
the source of an undead corruption. It 
is a threat to the village and must be 
dealt with. 
 
Within the Sunken Swamp the rooms 
are of varying heights and levels. Some 
rooms are open to the elements, the 
walls too high and wet to climb out of, 
while others turn downwards and 
appear as caves. The main structure 
was formed when an underground 
cavern collapsed on to a conduit of 
primordial magic. It prevented areas 
from filling with water, forming walls 
and open chambers, and revealing lost 
treasures. The presence of the magic 
imbued some of the creatures that 
lived there with an undead curse,

mutating some and empowering 
others, calling forth wraiths to possess 
long lost souls. 
 
Becoming Soaked 
 
Because you were more than aware of 
the wet nature of the marshes you 
have waterproofed your backpack with 
oil and fat but if the room you 
encounter is flooded you naturally 
acquire the soaked status. You have 
packed kindling and wood wrapped in 
oil cloth and can use this to create a 
fire. You have enough to start two fires 
in the Sunken Swamp and can only 
light them if the room description 
states there is a dry space. You will 
need to carefully monitor your soaked 
status. Don’t forget if you backtrack to 
a dry space you need to count the 
rooms you backtrack through. 
 
Flooded Rooms 
 
Some rooms in the Sunken Swamp are 
flooded and moving through them can 
be challenging. If you encounter two 
flooded chambers in a row you must 
roll on SOT1 the Soaked Table 1. This 
represents that potentially one or more 
of your items has been damaged. If 
the room is flooded it will be noted in 
the description. Small rooms do not 
count but large rooms do. 
 
Two New Runes 
 
There are two new Rune Stones in the 
Sunken Swamp. These runes draw 
upon the power of the ancient reptile 
god seen on numerous items and 
surfaces in the Lair. 
 
BOULTER’S SURGE RUNE  
 
Applied to Melee Weapons - Once per 
combat, after you have hit an 
opponent, you can fire a surge of 
water from the tip of your weapon, 
striking that opponent down, causing 
10 damage and knocking them to the 
floor. You can then follow up with an 
extra hit before the next round. 
 
ROMBOCO’S SCALE RUNE  
 
Applied to Melee Weapons - Once per 
combat as long as you are holding your 
weapon you can turn your skin to 
scales for an instant so as to deflect the 
strike. You take no damage this round.

share some of the same tables.

Note from the author 
 
“Thank you for playing the Lairs 
Expansion for 2D6 Dungeon. When I 
created this I wanted to offer up a 
different experience from the main 
human ancestry dungeon. I also 
wanted all adventurers, no matter what 
level they are, to be able to have a 
challenging and fair experience. So I 
had to have a leveling system built into 
the expansion which is not present in 
the main dungeon. I hope you enjoy  
what this offers.  
It is also worth nothing that it is fine if 
you want to tinker with some of these 
rules, house-ruling if you like. I have 
absolutely no problem with any player 
bringing their own ideas to the game. 
 
There will be a second Lairs volume, 
which will be an addon for the new 
2D6 Kingdom expansion Kickstarter 
campaign, coming later in 2023. So 
keep an eye out for more adventures. 
Don’t forget to also checkout our 
website at www.drgames.co.uk.” 
 
Enjoy! 
 
Toby Lancaster
